Actress and inventor: Hedy Lamarr.

Pioneer in wireless communications

About Hedy:

Personal information.

  • Name: Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler (AKA: Hedy Lamarr).
  • Born: november 9th, 1914. Viena, Austria.
  • Death: february 19th, 2000. Casselberry, Florida, EE. UU.
  • Profession: Actress and inventor.

Work and legacy.

  • During World War II, Lamarr and composer George Antheil devised a system to prevent military communications from being intercepted.
  • The system, called "frequency hopping", was based on changing the frequency of the radio signal at random.
  • Although the patent was granted in 1941, it was not adopted by the US military until the 1960s.
  • Frequency hopping technology is the basis of modern wireless communications such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth and GPS.
  • Despite her important contribution, Lamarr did not receive recognition for her invention until decades later.
  • In 1997, the year before her death, she was awarded the Pioneer Award by the Electronic Frontier Foundation.
  • In 2014, Google honoured her with a Doodle on the 100th anniversary of her birth.
  • Hedy Lamarr is an example of a pioneering woman who challenged the gender roles of her time.
  • Her invention has had a significant impact on the development of wireless communications.
  • Her story is an inspiration to women aspiring to careers in science, technology, engineering and mathematics.
